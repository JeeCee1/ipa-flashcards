About this app

An interactive flashcard and quiz tool for learning the phoneme inventory of Received Pronunciation (RP) — the reference accent of British English.

Cards cover all RP vowels, consonants, syllabic sonorants, suprasegmentals, and key morphophonological rules (-s, -ed endings, silent letters). The pronunciation quiz uses the word list from the textbook's appendix.

Course reference

Based on English Pronunciation for Teachers by Carlos Gussenhoven & Rian Broeders, 2nd edition (Martinus Nijhoff / Wolters-Noordhoff). Phonemic inventory, rules, exceptions, and word list follow that book's conventions.

Transcriptions follow the Longman Pronunciation Dictionary (J.C. Wells), with the four LPD conventions not used in this course noted on the LPD card.

Phonetic descriptions

Each card's formal name (e.g. Close back rounded — long) is a label read directly off the IPA chart by cross-referencing row (tongue height: close / open-mid…), column (backness: front / central / back), rounding, and length. These formal labels do not appear as such in G&B — G&B describes each sound in prose using the same dimensions, but does not present them as named IPA labels.

The short descriptions (e.g. Long tense vowel. Tongue high-back, lips rounded.) are Claude.ai paraphrases of articulatory descriptions given in G&B, consistent with standard IPA terminology. They are not verbatim quotations.

See-also badge legend

Each See also button on a card back carries a small badge indicating where that relationship comes from.

G&B
Gussenhoven & Broeders — the relationship is explicitly stated or clearly implied in the course textbook.
GPF
General phonological fact — a standard phonological grouping (e.g. both are centring diphthongs) that is not specific to this textbook but is uncontroversial in the field.
CAI
Claude.ai editorial — a pedagogically useful grouping added by Claude.ai, not directly traceable to any source. Treat with appropriate scepticism.
